Place / content
Graphic design creates experiences. It’s a way of story telling that grasps the attention of the viewer and communicates with. Creating a message on a location has the power to completely change the context of the work.
In the art world we have a lot of artists that make site-specific art works that work really well. I’m curious if we can do the same as graphic designers. How does this translation work? Can we communicate more efficient and create better storytelling by using the surroundings a message is in?
The content of a work is being influenced by a lot of factors; its aesthetics, surroundings, time, experience and the input of the viewer. I would like to focus on the form and surroundings a work has.
The meaning of space is also changing because of technology. Because of new media and the format of video and web, the things we design don’t have anymore one specific place. They are supposed to work for different formats and places.
